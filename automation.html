The Approach

Smarter operations, fewer errors.

Automation Solutions at TIA Consulting are about finding the right things to automate...not automating everything. The goal is to identify the repetitive, error-prone, or time-consuming tasks in your operations and replace them with reliable, documented workflows that run without constant human oversight.

I work with the tools your organization already uses, or help you find the right ones. Automations are built to be transparent and maintainable, not black boxes your team doesn't understand. When I hand something off, you know how it works and what to do if something breaks.

The Process

How automations get built.

Every automation engagement follows the same grounded process — so what gets built is reliable, not just impressive.

  1. 01

    Discovery Call

    We talk through what's happening manually, what's breaking, and where time is being lost. I ask about your tools, your team, and your tolerance for change.

  2. 02

    Workflow Mapping

    I map the current process in detail, so we know exactly what we're replacing and why.

  3. 03

    Automation Design

    I design the automation logic before building anything and share it with you for review. No surprises when the build begins.

  4. 04

    Build & Test

    I build the automation and test it against real-world scenarios, including edge cases and failure modes so it works when it matters.

  5. 05

    Handoff & Documentation

    You receive clear documentation, a walkthrough of how it works, and guidance on what to monitor or adjust over time.

Is this right for you?

Automation for the right reasons.

A good fit if…

  • Your team regularly does the same manual steps every day or week
  • Data gets entered in multiple places and they don't always match
  • Things routinely slip through the cracks because of handoff failures
  • You've thought about automating but weren't sure what was possible
  • You want something your team understands and can maintain
  • You want your humans to focus on what they do best rather than repetitive tasks

May not be the right fit if…

  • You want automation to replace human judgment, not repetitive tasks
  • You're not willing to document or change the underlying process
  • You need large-scale enterprise integration engineering
  • Your goals involve automating targeting, surveillance, or harm
